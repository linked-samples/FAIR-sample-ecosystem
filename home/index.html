Open FAIR Samples

Open and FAIR samples: maturing the sample data ecosystem through coordinated guidance, persistent identifiers, and leading metadata practices.

Ways to Engage:

  • Sign up for project updates to hear about upcoming workshops, hackathons, and focus groups
  • Participate in a survey or focus group about sample data practices in your community
  • Find us at an upcoming conference

Challenge

Scientific samples are difficult to discover, cite, and connect with related research outputs. Fragmented infrastructure and inconsistent adoption of persistent identifiers limit sample FAIRness. Key stakeholders — researchers, data repositories, sample repositories, and scholarly publishers — lack coordinated guidance for implementing open sample data practices.

Technical Approach

The Open FAIR Samples project will develop four community roadmaps — one each with researchers, data repositories, sample repositories, and scholarly publishers — to advance the adoption of persistent identifiers and leading metadata practices for scientific samples.

We apply participatory design and social science methods to understand and improve stakeholder workflows, and leverage existing NSF infrastructure and community networks to foster scalable and sustainable implementation.

Broader Impacts

  • Enhance the long-term value of NSF investments in national sample collections
  • Support FAIR samples and reproducibility in sample-based research
  • Empower researcher alignment with NSF directives promoting sample metadata archiving and reuse

Key Deliverables

  • Four community roadmaps — co-developed with researchers, data repositories, sample repositories, and scholarly publishers
  • Undergraduate and graduate educational modules and peer-reviewed publications, co-authored with community
  • Hackathons, surveys, workshops, and focus groups to prototype, socialize, and advance development and adoption of open science infrastructure for sample-based research
