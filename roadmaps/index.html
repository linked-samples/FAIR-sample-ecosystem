Community Roadmaps

We are co-developing four roadmaps — one for each key stakeholder group — to coordinate and advance the adoption of persistent identifiers and leading metadata practices for scientific samples.

The Four Roadmaps

Each roadmap is being developed through participatory design and social science methods in direct collaboration with stakeholder communities. The roadmaps will provide coordinated guidance tailored to each group's workflows and needs.

🔬

Researchers

Guidance for researchers on citing samples, using persistent identifiers (PIDs), and connecting samples with related research outputs and publications.

Activities: Four-semester cohort program, educational module development.

Planning
💾

Data Repositories

Guidance for data repositories on supporting sample PIDs, improving metadata standards, and connecting sample data across systems.

Activities: Data repository-focused webinar series, hybrid-format hackathons.

Planning
🏛

Sample Repositories

Guidance for sample repositories on adopting and supporting persistent identifiers, improving interoperability, and aligning with community metadata standards.

Activities: Landscape survey of U.S. ESS sample repositories, case studies of repository realities and workflows.

Planning
📚

Scholarly Publishers

Guidance for publishers on supporting sample citation in Earth and space sciences, integrating sample PIDs into publishing workflows, and promoting open sample data.

Activities: Assessing the state of sample citation in ESS scholarly publishing, focus groups about supporting sample PIDs.

Engagement Activities

The roadmaps are being developed through a range of community engagement activities. These activities will be announced as they are scheduled — sign up for updates to participate.

📋

Surveys

Community-wide surveys to understand current sample data practices, pain points, and needs across stakeholder groups.

🎤

Workshops

Virtual and in-person workshops to co-develop roadmap content and prototype solutions with community members.

💻

Hackathons

Hybrid-format hackathons to advance development of open science infrastructure for sample-based research.

💬

Focus Groups

Targeted focus groups with specific stakeholder communities to deeply understand workflows and inform roadmap design.

The Open FAIR Samples project is funded by the U.S. National Science Foundation under the Findable Accessible Interoperable Reusable Open Science (FAIROS) program: "Disciplinary Improvements: Open and FAIR samples: maturing the sample data ecosystem".
